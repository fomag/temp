The normal period for individuals and graduated rate estates is:

  • within three years of the end of the tax year to claim a tax refund
  • within three years of the date of the original notice of assessment to request a reassessment

If the income tax return(s) were not previously filed, individuals and graduated rate estates can request a refund by filing them. Send the income tax returns and supporting documentation to the tax office where you file your returns.

  • Individuals: Go to www.cra.gc.ca/tso for Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) tax centre addresses
  • Graduated rate estates: Go to Where to file a T3 return for the correct address

If the income tax return was previously filed:

  • For individual accounts, go to How to change your return for instructions on submitting your adjustment request.
  • For graduated rate estates, send us a completed Form T3-ADJ, T3 Adjustment Request, or letter providing the details of the change. Indicate the trust‚Äôs account number, the tax year you want to change, and attach all supporting documents. For details about where to send your letter or completed form, see Form T3-ADJ, T3 Adjustment Request.

The normal period for a corporation:

  • to request a reassessment is within three years of the date we sent the original notice of assessment for the tax year, if the corporation was a Canadian-controlled private corporation at the end of the year or within four years if it was not a Canadian-controlled private corporation at the end of the year
  • to claim a tax refund is within three years of the end of the tax year

Go to Requesting a reassessment of your T2 retur for details on how to submit a reassessment request beyond the normal period.

Go to Re-appropriation of T2 statute-barred credits for details on how to claim a tax refund beyond the normal period.

Refund interest charged against your account represents interest we paid to you on a refund you're no longer entitled to. You cannot ask for relief of this recovery charge.

You can send your request for an extension to file a return to the tax centre that serves you. See Tax services offices and tax centres to determine where to send your request.

Once you've received your notice of assessment, you can ask for relief. If you still need to file a return or an adjustment to a return, submit your relief request along with the return or adjustment request. Go to Submitting your request for instructions on preparing your relief request.

You can ask us to apply your amount to a different year or period. See Tax services offices and tax centres to determine which tax centre to send your request to

If your return was filed on time, request a reassessment by writing in to the tax centre that serves your area. See Tax services offices and tax centres to determine which tax centre to send your request to. Include any proof you may have with your request such as tracking information for your mail or parcel.
